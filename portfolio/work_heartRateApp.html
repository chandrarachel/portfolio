PulseRePLY

Wearable Heart Rate Monitoring System

Role: Mobile App Developer (React Native)

"After noticing dizziness at home, Alex uses the PulseRePLY app. When his resting heart rate spikes, he gets an instant visual alert and logs his symptoms. The app summarizes his patterns and generates a PDF report, helping his doctor intervene early."

Project Overview

I developed a prototype mobile app for a wearable heart rate monitoring system as my final project for the Software Engineering course at KAIST. Collaborating with an international team, I gained fresh perspectives and honed my ability to work with people from diverse backgrounds. Our focus was on delivering a seamless user experience and creating real value for users by addressing health monitoring needs.

Key Accomplishments

Designed and implemented a mobile app using React Native, featuring:

  • Real-time heart rate and activity tracking (simulated data)
  • Immediate visual alerts for abnormal heart rates
  • Symptom logging with notes for context
  • Automatic summaries and trend reporting (daily/weekly summaries)
  • PDF export of health reports for doctor consultations

Collaborative development:

  • Worked closely with teammates from different countries and backgrounds
  • Maintained open communication and frequently clarified requirements to ensure alignment
Photo: Presenting PulseRePLY at Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology

What I Learned

  • Cross-cultural teamwork: Collaborated with an international team, gaining fresh perspectives and stronger ideas
  • Communication & delegation: Improved at clear communication, task delegation, and leveraging team strengths.
  • User-focused problem solving: Learned to identify real user needs and build solutions that deliver genuine value, especially in health tech.

Tech Stack

React Native (iOS & Android) • Expo • Figma (UI design) • simulated BLE integration
